My Development Cycle

  • Discovery and Planning

    First meeting | Communication | "What are you looking for?"

    First things first, when developing any software application, it's important to come together and gather information on the topic at hand. Whether that be a brand new website or webpage tune up. It's important to gather a lot of information and establish good communication practices right at the beginning. Good communication in the beginning gives me a better picture of what is needed later down the line. The better my understanding, the better the finished product.

  • Design, Design, Design

    Inital Webpage Ideas | Visual Representation

    Creating designs behind the website helps me to visualize the project at hand. Coming at it with designs will allow me to see exactly what you want. I could then give my professional opinion and we compare and contrast ideas to make your page both functional and appealing.

  • Code Implementation

    Building the Software | Creating and Adding Features

    Time for my skills to really shine! Writing the code can be a grueling task and patience is always appreciated. Implementation is the bulk of the programming, however there is still much to be done after this step.

  • Testing and Feedback

    Changes and Adaptation | "What do you think so far?"

    With new software development there are always some bugs and features that may not work as intended. This is why it's important to run through testing all aspects of the software to identify bugs and correct them. Feedback is also crucial as post implementation you may want certain pages changed or have different features in mind. Applying my code to your vision of success is my main goal.

  • Deployment and Maintenance

    Hosting Consultation | Maintenance | Updates

    Finally, we deploy your software and host it on the web. It's always nice to update and change information on your pages as it offers new content to be explored by users. Modernizing portions and making small changes could impact the user perceptions of your site in the long term.
